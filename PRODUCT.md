# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Casual club tennis players — weekend warriors, social hitters, recreational competitors. They open the app right after a session to quickly log what happened: who they played, how matches went, what they drilled. Context: on a phone, court-side or in the car, still in tennis gear. Primary task on any given screen is either "log this session fast" or "browse past sessions."

## Product Purpose

Evenstar is a personal tennis session journal. It exists to help club players track their game over time — not optimize performance scientifically, but remember sessions, celebrate wins, and notice patterns. Success is the player opening the app after every session because it takes less than a minute to log.

## Brand Personality

Fun, personal, social. The feel of a classic membership register crossed with a sporty, energetic app — like a tennis club's logbook that doesn't take itself too seriously.

## Anti-references

- Not Strava or clinical fitness trackers (no dashboard overload, no stats worship)
- Not dark/black-mode obsessed (Hevy's aesthetic is a reference for structure, not its color mood)
- Not corporate SaaS blue
- Not generic white-and-card CRUD app

## Design Principles

1. **Log fast, log often.** Every flow should be completable in under 60 seconds. Speed and friction reduction are the top priorities.
2. **The court, not the spreadsheet.** Data should feel human and story-like, not analytical. A session is a memory, not a record.
3. **Sporty confidence.** The interface should have the energy of someone who plays regularly — decisive, comfortable, unhurried.
4. **Earn the character.** The classic English club aesthetic earns its place only where it adds warmth. It should never get in the way of the task.
5. **Familiar affordances, distinctive skin.** Standard patterns (lists, tabs, forms) with a personality. Don't reinvent; embellish.
